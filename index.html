About the role

Where industrial design meets electrical engineering

We are looking for a Mechanical Design Engineer with a proven track record in electronic enclosure design. In this role, you are expected to bridge the gap between industrial design and electrical engineering. You will design the physical architecture of our electronic devices, ensuring that internal components — PCBs, batteries, connectors, and displays — fit safely, look sleek, and survive real-world environments.

You will guide designs from initial concept and 3D modeling through rapid prototyping (3D printing, laser cutting) to final mass production optimization.

Key responsibilities

Your bill of materials for the role

Seven line items, one enclosure. Here's what you'll own end to end.

ITEM 01

Enclosure Design

Create production-ready 3D CAD models of enclosures using SolidWorks or AutoCAD, balancing aesthetics, structural integrity, and ease of assembly.

ITEM 02

Component Integration

Collaborate closely with electrical engineers to import PCB layouts, connectors, and batteries into CAD systems to ensure perfect mechanical clearance and fit.

ITEM 03

Prototyping & Testing

Rapidly iterate designs using in-house fabrication tools like 3D printing (FDM/SLA) and laser cutting to test form, fit, and function.

ITEM 04

Thermal & Mechanical Simulation

Perform Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and thermal simulations to guarantee the enclosure protects against drops, impacts, and overheating.

ITEM 05

Design for Manufacturing (DFM)

Optimize parts for mass production processes, specifically injection molding, sheet metal fabrication, or CNC machining.

ITEM 06

Environmental Protection

Incorporate gaskets, seals, and interlocking joints to meet specific Ingress Protection (IP) ratings.

ITEM 07

Documentation

Create detailed 2D engineering drawings with precise tolerances, bill of materials (BOM), and assembly instructions.

Required skills & qualifications

What you'll bring to the bench

Education

Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering, Product Design Engineering, or a related field.

Software Expertise

Advanced proficiency in SolidWorks (highly preferred) or AutoCAD, including sheet metal, plastics, and surfacing features.

Electronics Packaging Experience

Demonstrated experience designing enclosures for PCBs, managing tolerance stack-ups, and integrating hardware components.

Prototyping Skills

Hands-on experience operating and troubleshooting 3D printers and laser cutters.

Manufacturing Knowledge

Strong understanding of DFM/DFA rules, draft angles, wall thickness constraints, and material selection (ABS, Polycarbonate, Aluminum, etc.).

Problem-Solving

Ability to optimize designs for specific environmental constraints.
